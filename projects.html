Master Thesis (2017)

Topic: Predicting student's academic performance in a Machine Learning Approach.

I worked with several academics from the Institute for Teaching and Learning Innovation (ITaLI) at University of Queensland to develop a student dashboard. My individual goal is to build up a systematic student score prediction algorithm from scratch, with the application of Machine Learning techniques. The team has to meet regularly in order to keep our working progress on track, thus, professional communications and presentations were required. Eventually I have to deliver a well-documented thesis to report on my significant findings, algorithm development process and reasoning. I obtained substantial amount of data mining knowledge and practical experiences in this project. 

Data Mining Project (2017)

This is an interesting project done throughout the last semester in Data Mining course. Within a small team, we have to select a dataset by ourselves then apply data mining techniques to gain insights into this dataset (we have chosen the US Census dataset in 1996, with about 300,000 samples in total). Among other teams, we were one of the few selected teams to present our findings in the lecture. I am responsible for applying unsupervised association rules mining to discover the interesting hidden patterns in this dataset; unexpected discoveries such as gender inequality in terms of high income, and some other rules were found. I learned techniques in tunning parameters and selecting appropriate evaluation statistics in this project.
